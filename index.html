Using cloud init to configure your VMs

Cloud-init is the defacto multi-distribution package that handles early initialization of a cloud instance. It is supported by all major cloud providers, so it was a natural fit for our multicloud solution. It provides an easy way to configure your VMs upon initialization, allowing you to set up your environment in a fast, easy and reusable way. When launching a server in Mist.io, you now have the option to provide a cloud-config file as long as the provider and OS supports it. Not sure of what you can achieve with cloud-init? You can check out the docs or our blog post on [LINK] how to launch a Kubernetes development environment with one click

Tagging and grouping your infrastructure

Mist.io now supports a key:value mechanism for tagging your VMs and containers. It can help you group your infrastructure in a powerful and flexible way and access or manage them in bulk. When supported by the cloud provider it will also propagate this tags to it, otherwise it will store them locally, to providing tagging and grouping features even when the provider does not support them.

Support for Packet.net

Packet.net is out to build a better internet by supercharging the container revolution with smart, API-driven bare metal. Their platform brings the price and performance benefits of bare metal servers to the cloud, powering highly-available performance workloads through a unique, never-congested network. We love what they are doing and we decided to [LINK] add support for Packet.net at Mist.io in our quest to provide as many options as possible to our users.
